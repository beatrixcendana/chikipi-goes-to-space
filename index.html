Chikipi's Journey to Space

Follow Chikipi the curious chicken on an extraordinary adventure to the stars!

üé• The Dream Begins

One day, Chikipi the chicken was watching a YouTube video about the International Space Station (ISS). His little eyes grew wide with wonder as he saw astronauts floating in space, doing incredible science experiments, and looking down at Earth from space!

"I want to go there too!" chirped Chikipi excitedly. He learned that the ISS orbits Earth at 408 kilometers above us, traveling at an amazing speed of 28,000 km/h!

ü™ü The Amazing Cupola

Chikipi discovered something magical called the Cupola - a special room on the ISS with seven windows! It's like a observatory in space where astronauts can see Earth, stars, and even watch spacewalks.

"I must see that view!" thought Chikipi. But first, he needed training...

üèä Training at the NBL

Chikipi learned that astronauts train at the Neutral Buoyancy Laboratory (NBL) - a giant swimming pool that's as deep as a 12-story building! This special pool helps astronauts practice for spacewalks.

The NBL pool contains 23.5 million liters of water and is 12 meters deep! Being underwater feels similar to floating in space (microgravity), so it's perfect for training.

üåü Chikipi's Dream Comes True!

After all his training, Chikipi finally made it to the ISS! He floated through the Cupola's windows, watching Earth spin below with its blue oceans, green forests, and swirling white clouds.

"This is the most amazing view in the universe!" chirped Chikipi, doing a happy zero-gravity flip. He proved that with curiosity, determination, and hard work, anyone can reach for the stars!
