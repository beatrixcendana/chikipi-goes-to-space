Chikipi's Journey to Space

Chikipi, a small and plastic chicken, has left the cozy confines of his farm to take on the biggest challenge of his life: becoming an extraordinary astronaut trainer!

🎥 The Dream Begins

One day, Chikipi the chicken was watching a YouTube video about the International Space Station (ISS). His little eyes sparkled with wonder as he saw astronauts floating in space, doing incredible science experiments, and looking down at Earth from space!

"I want to go there too!" chirped Chikipi excitedly. He learned that the ISS orbits Earth at 408 kilometers above us, traveling at an amazing speed of 28,000 km/h!

🪟 The Amazing Cupola

He discovered something truly magical: the Cupola. "It's like a floating ball with seven windows, but in space!" he exclaimed. The Cupola is a special room that allows you to see whole entire panoramic view from stars, moon, and even spacewalking astronauts. This is the astronauts' favorite spot to take stunning photographs of our beautiful blue planet.

7 Windows

The Cupola has the largest windows ever in space!

Full Circle View

See Earth, stars, and space from every angle!

Best Seat

Astronauts' favorite place to take photos of Earth!

"I must see that view!" thought Chikipi. But first, he needed training...

🏊 Training at the NBL

Chikipi quickly figured out that space is not just for playing around. Astronauts need serious practice, which led him to NASA’s secret weapon in Houston: the Neutral Buoyancy Laboratory (NBL). "Wait, is that a pool... or a lake?" Chikipi squawked.

The NBL pool contains 23.5 million liters of water and is 12 meters deep! Being underwater feels similar to floating in space or we call it microgravity, so it's perfect for training.

Size matters!

🎈 Experiencing No Gravity

If Chikipi can fly in the sky, now he can experience floating or microgravity inside the station! Almost no gravity, even water droplets dance in the air!

✨ No Up or Down

In space, any direction can be "up"!

🌟 Chikipi's Dream Comes True!

Chikipi achieved his goal: to be an extraordinary astronaut trainer! He imagined himself from the magical window, looking down at Earth, knowing that he helped future crews prepare for the real thing.

"This is the most amazing view in the universe!" chirped Chikipi, doing a happy zero-gravity flip.
